# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud



UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive information. With the rapid growth of digital payments in India, such frauds have increased significantly.



## How the Fraud Happens



A typical UPI fraud begins when a victim receives a call, SMS, or message claiming to be from a bank representative, delivery service, or customer support. The fraudster creates urgency by saying there is an issue with the user’s account or a pending reward. The victim is then asked to click on a malicious link or accept a “collect request” on a UPI app. In some cases, they are instructed to install screen-sharing applications. Once the victim follows these steps, the fraudster tricks them into approving a payment or gains access to their account, leading to an instant transfer of money.



## Example Scenario



For example, a college student received a call from someone pretending to be a bank official who claimed that his account needed urgent verification. He was asked to approve a UPI request sent to his phone. Trusting the caller, he approved it, and ₹5,000 was immediately deducted from his account.



## Targets and Consequences 



Such scams usually target students, elderly people, and individuals who are not fully aware of digital payment systems. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and possible misuse of personal banking information. Since UPI transactions are instant, recovering lost money is often difficult.
